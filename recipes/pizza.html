Turkish Pizza Recipes

Description

Lahmacun, also known as Turkish pizza, is a thin, round flatbread topped with a flavorful mixture of ground meat, vegetables, herbs, and spices. Baked until crisp and served hot with lemon and fresh parsley, it's a popular street food in Turkey—rolled up and eaten by hand. It's light, spicy, and incredibly satisfying.


Ingredients

  • 250g (1/2 lb) ground lamb or beef
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped or grated
  • 1 small green pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 thin flatbreads or pizza bases (or homemade dough)
  • Lemon wedges and fresh parsley (for serving)

How to prepare it?

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Prepare the topping: In a bowl, mix ground meat with chopped onion, tomato, pepper, garlic, tomato paste, parsley, spices, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Mix thoroughly into a spreadable paste.
  3. Assemble the lahmacun: Place flatbreads on baking sheets. Spread a thin layer of meat mixture evenly over each one, pressing it down.
  4. Bake: Cook for 8–10 minutes until the edges are crisp and the meat is cooked through.
  5. Serve: Garnish with lemon wedges and fresh parsley. Serve rolled or flat, optionally with sliced onions and sumac.
